Expression vs. Statement

Dewey’s distinction between artistic representation and ordinary representation

1. Theory

Aristotle

  • Poetry → universals
  • History → particulars

Dewey

  • Statement → generalized
  • Expression → individualized

2. Example (Grief)

Statement

  • general symbol of sadness
  • type of emotion

Expression

  • grief of a particular individual
  • connected with a particular event

3. Conclusion

A work of art must present a subject matter in such a way that the emotional and attitudinal significance of it can be explored through interaction with the work itself.

Success in artistic representation is indissolubly wedded to the explorative working of the medium and the achievement of expressiveness.
